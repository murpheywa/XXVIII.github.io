The XXVIII Professional Tour

Scores and statistics as of September 14, 2023

Overview

We are tracking our Wordle games as if we were all touring PGA or LIV golf pros. Following in the footsteps of "LIV Golf", I named our tour "The XXVIII" since each of our tournaments consist of 28 holes (see below).

Tour Details

  • Each Wordle game represents a single golf hole. Each guess in a game is a single golf stroke.
  • Each week (starting on Sunday) represents a single round of seven (7) holes.
  • Each calendar year is a golf "season". We divide the season into 13 tournaments of four rounds each (four calendar weeks). The tournament names you see in the scoreboards below roughly coincide with a PGA tournament played at that time of year.
  • The leaderboard, tournament statistics, season statistics, and scorecards are captured and presented below for each tournament in each season.

Scoring Details

  • Each guess represents a single golf stroke.
  • The par for each hole is four (4).
  • The maximum score for any hole is seven (7).
  • If you miss a hole ("DNP" column on the scorecard), you are charged seven strokes.
  • You have seven (7) days to record each hole via our Wordle SMS (text) chat group. After that, you are assessed seven strokes.
  • A four-week tournament has twenty-eight holes. Tournament par is 112 strokes.

In golf, people pay particular attention to two additional metrics: reaching the green "in regulation" (two strokes on a par four hole), and the number of putts taken. In golf, "par" is calculated as the sum of reaching the green "in regulation" plus two for putts.

In The XXVIII, we record those statistics as follows:

  • When you get at least one correct letter in the correct spot (i.e., a green Wordle tile), you are considered "on the green". Additional guesses are recorded as putts.
    • For example, if the "Greens In Reg." column below shows "80%", that means the player had at least one correct guess in the correct spot on or before the second turn on 80% of the Wordle games.

Statistics Details

If you are familiar at all with golf scoring, the statistics below should be intuitively obvious. Below are a few specific clarifications.

The Leaderboard

  • The "DNP" column is the number of Wordle games that the player skipped (or failed to turn in a scorecard). This column also appears on the scorecards and tourney/season statistics grids.
  • The "Total" column is the number of strokes +/- par in the tournament.
  • The "Round" column is the number of strokes +/- par in the current round.

Tournament and Season Statistics

  • The season "Scoring Avg" column is the average number of strokes +/- par based on a 28-hole tournament. This is the only statistic that includes only the actual strokes recorded. In other words, the "DNP" holes are excluded from this statistic.
  • A number in parentheses in the Tourney and Round wins columns indicates how many of those wins were ties. For example, "3 (2)" means one solo win and and two ties.
  • The "Streak" column in the Season statistics indicates the player's longest participation streak during the season. The number in parenthesis is the length of the current participation streak.
  • The "Putting Avg" column shows the average number of putts as described above per hole. No putts are recorded when the player fails to guess a word correctly, fails to play a hole, or fails to report a score.
  • The "Greens In Reg" (regulation) column show the percent of holes that the player reached the green in regulation as describe above.

Epilogue

Hopefully you'll find this scoring makes playing Wordle in our group more enjoyable. Feel free to email or call me with complaints, suggestions. etc.

Have fun, and be sure to tip the beverage cart operator.
